Executive Summary

We propose establishing a dedicated AI education server at NAS Jiaxing — a local, privacy-focused platform that gives teachers and students direct access to cutting-edge AI tools without relying on external cloud services or VPNs.

The Problem We Are Solving

Teachers want to use AI in classrooms, but face three barriers:

  1. Access blocked — Many AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) are blocked on school networks without VPN
  2. Data privacy — Uploading student work to foreign cloud services raises compliance concerns
  3. Cost and complexity — Premium AI subscriptions are expensive; teachers don't know where to start

Our Solution

A local AI server running inside the school network, hosting open-source AI models and educational tools that are:

  • Always accessible — No VPN needed; works on school WiFi
  • Fully private — All data stays inside the school; no external cloud uploads
  • Free to use — Open-source software; no subscription fees
  • Educational — Designed specifically for teaching, not general chat

Why We Recommend the Standard Tier (A1 Rackmount, ~¥12,050)

For a school environment with a server room, the Standard tier is the best long-term investment:

  • Enterprise form factor — 2U rackmount sits in the server room, not on a desk. Professional, secure, out of the way.
  • Redundant power — dual 2200W hot-swap PSUs. If one fails, the other keeps running. Zero downtime for students.
  • 10GbE networking — dual SFP+ ports onboard. Future-proof when the school upgrades network infrastructure. 10x faster than 1GbE for large AI model transfers.
  • Maximum expansion — 10 PCIe slots (can add 2–4 more Tesla A2s later), 8 hot-swap drive bays (add storage without opening the case).
  • Enterprise reliability — built for 24/7 datacenter operation. Full-tower cases with tower coolers are not designed for sustained 100% load.
  • 32 cores is enough — AI inference is GPU-bound, not CPU-bound. The Tesla A2s will be the bottleneck long before 32 EPYC cores max out.
  • Clear upgrade path — Start with Entry (~¥8,850) for a pilot, then add RAM and a second GPU to reach Standard (~¥12,050) when proven.

When to choose Entry tier: If the school wants to start small and validate the concept before full deployment. Entry gives the same rackmount chassis, redundancy, and 10GbE — just less RAM and one GPU. Upgrade later without replacing hardware.

If no server room is available: A full-tower dual-CPU workstation (~¥13,650, 64 cores, 2x Tesla A2) is an alternative. It delivers more CPU cores for only ¥1,600 more than Standard, but loses rackmount, redundant PSU, and 10GbE. Best for offices or classrooms where rackmount is impossible. We are committed to full compliance with Chinese regulations:

  • ICP备案 — The site will operate under the school's existing ICP filing as a subdomain (ai.nasjiaxing.cn)
  • Data Sovereignty — All data stored locally; nothing sent to foreign servers

  • No VPN Required — Everything runs on the school internal network
  • Firewall Friendly — Standard HTTP on port 80; no special ports or protocols needed
