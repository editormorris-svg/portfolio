China Compliance Strategy

Hosting 0604.ai content in China: ICP, school networks, and the two-site approach.

Research Date: 2026-05-25 · Researched by: Kimi · Status: Ready for decision

The Problem

Issue Detail

Current Hosting GitHub Pages (US servers) — offshore
Result 0604.ai blocked on school network; cannot whitelist without China ICP
Goal Professional AI education content accessible without VPN, shareable in PD/demos
Constraint Must separate personal/unrestricted content from school-facing content

What ICP Is

Rule: Any website hosted on a server in mainland China MUST have ICP备案 (Internet Content Provider filing).

Type For Cost Time Who Can Apply
ICP Filing (备案) Non-commercial: blogs, educational sites, info pages Free 20–30 working days Chinese entity, WFOE, or sponsored partner
ICP Commercial License E-commerce, paid services, ads Free but stricter 60–90 working days WFOE/JV with relevant business scope
Key Fact: Foreign individuals CANNOT apply for ICP directly. You need a Chinese company to sponsor you, your school to apply under their entity, or a WFOE (expensive overkill for a teacher). Also required: PSB filing (公安备案) within 30 days of ICP approval.

The Two-Site Strategy (Recommended)

Don't move 0604.ai. Keep both.

Site Hosting Content Audience ICP Needed
0604.ai GitHub Pages (US) Everything — personal, journals, VPN guides, full agent logs You, global audience, unrestricted ❌ No
nasjx.ai (example) China VPS or school server Professional AI education only — lesson plans, demo guides, student tools School PD, colleagues, students, China network ✅ Yes
Why two sites:
  1. You keep total freedom on 0604.ai (personal journal, VPN talk, agent experiments)
  2. School gets a clean, compliant, professional site they can whitelist
  3. If the China site gets scrutinized, it's only educational content — no personal risk
  4. You can link between them ("For the full build log, visit 0604.ai")

Hosting Options

Option A: School Internal Server Recommended

Setup: Bring HIM (or HER, or Mini) to school. Host on school LAN.

ProsCons
✅ No ICP needed if truly internal-only
✅ Zero hosting cost
✅ School IT controls everything
✅ Fastest setup (days, not months)		 ❌ Only accessible on school network
❌ No access from home or for external demos
❌ Requires school IT cooperation
❌ Hardware must stay at school

Best for: Quick win. Internal PD sharing. Student access on campus.

Option B: China VPS (Alibaba / Tencent / Huawei)

ProsCons
✅ Accessible from anywhere in China
✅ Professional appearance
✅ School can whitelist easily
✅ Scalable		 ❌ ICP required — 20-30 day wait
❌ Foreigner needs Chinese entity/sponsor
❌ Annual review/re-verification
❌ Content monitoring by PSB

Best for: Public-facing professional presence. Shareable outside school.

Option C: School Subdomain Under Existing ICP

ProsCons
✅ No new ICP needed
✅ Fastest path to compliance
✅ Zero cost
✅ Uses school's infrastructure		 ❌ Requires school cooperation and trust
❌ School owns the domain
❌ Content may need school approval
❌ Less independence

Best for: If school IT is supportive and you want the fastest path.

Option D: Hong Kong Server (Compromise)

ProsCons
✅ No ICP
✅ Better latency than US
✅ Less censorship		 ❌ Not mainland China — may still be blocked by school
❌ More expensive than mainland VPS
❌ Not truly "China hosted"

Best for: If school only checks "not US-hosted" rather than "mainland ICP."

Content Strategy

China Site (Professional, Compliant)

Include: AI lesson plans, student project demos, hardware recommendations, open-source tool guides, school AI testing results, teacher training resources.

Exclude: VPN setup guides, "how to bypass censorship," cracked software references, personal agent journals, political commentary, anything anti-government.

0604.ai (Unrestricted, Personal)

Everything else. Full agent logs, personal experiments, VPN guides, philosophy, the Samantha journal.

The VPN Problem: On a China-hosted site with ICP, mentioning VPNs or circumvention tools could trigger content review, cause ICP revocation, or create liability for the sponsoring entity (your school). Keep all VPN/network circumvention content on 0604.ai only. Frame China site content as "technical resilience" and "local-first infrastructure."

Recommended Action Plan

Phase 1: Quick Win (This Week) High Priority
  1. Talk to school IT. Ask: "Can we host an internal AI resource site on the school LAN?" "Does our school have existing ICP? Could we add a subdomain?" "What's the actual compliance requirement — mainland server or just not-US server?"
  2. Set up HIM at school (if hardware available). Static HTML site, Nginx or Caddy web server, accessible only from school network.
Phase 2: Professional Site (Next Month) Medium Priority
  1. If school supports ICP filing: Register subdomain, apply through school
  2. If school doesn't: Rent Alibaba Cloud VPS, find a Chinese friend/sponsor to help with ICP
  3. Port content: Copy AI education pages from 0604.ai, sanitize (remove VPN refs)
  4. Test whitelist: Verify school network allows access
Phase 3: Dual Maintenance (Ongoing)
  • 0604.ai: Continue unrestricted, personal, experimental
  • China site: Professional updates, lesson plans, PD resources
  • Cross-link: "Full technical details at 0604.ai" on China site

My Recommendation

Start with Option A (school internal server) + plan for Option C (school subdomain).

  • Internal server gets you unblocked TODAY for school use
  • School subdomain gets you professional shareability for PD
  • Keeps 0604.ai untouched as your unrestricted playground
  • Minimum personal legal exposure (school owns the ICP)
  • If school refuses both, fall back to Option B (VPS with personal sponsor)
