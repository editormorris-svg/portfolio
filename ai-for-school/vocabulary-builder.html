The Three-Tier Vocabulary System

Not all words are created equal. The Three-Tier system helps EAL students focus their limited cognitive resources on the words that matter most:

Tier 1 Basic Vocabulary

What: High-frequency words used in everyday speech (happy, walk, house)

For EAL: Often familiar from spoken language; focus on spelling and academic usage

beautiful slowly important friend story

Tier 2 General Academic Vocabulary ⭐ PRIORITY

What: Words that appear across subjects but aren't common in conversation (analyze, demonstrate, consequence)

For EAL: Highest ROI for instruction—unlocks comprehension across all subjects

significant investigate establish previous predict complex

Tier 3 Domain-Specific Vocabulary

What: Content-specific words (photosynthesis, metaphor, democracy)

For EAL: Teach as needed for the unit; often cognates exist in other languages

alliteration ecosystem revolution photosynthesis
💡 The 80/20 Rule for EAL Vocabulary: Focus 80% of your explicit instruction on Tier 2 words. These 2,000-3,000 words account for the majority of academic text complexity. Native speakers acquire these gradually; EAL students need intentional support.

The 7-Step Vocabulary Instruction Sequence

Research shows EAL students need 12-14 meaningful exposures to truly acquire a new word. Here's a systematic approach:

Step Activity Time Example
1 Contextualize 2 min Show word in original text context
2 Define 1 min Student-friendly definition (avoid dictionary)
3 Visualize 2 min Image, gesture, or diagram
4 Connect 2 min Cognates, synonyms, word families
5 Process 3 min Turn and talk, write sentence
6 Consolidate 5 min Game, sorting, or matching activity
7 Review 2 min Spaced repetition in later lessons

Vocabulary Activity Templates

Activity 1: Frayer Model (Visual Organizer)

📋 How to Use

Divide a page into 4 quadrants around a target word:

  • Top Left: Definition (student's own words)
  • Top Right: Characteristics (What is it like?)
  • Bottom Left: Examples
  • Bottom Right:>Non-examples

AI Prompt: "Create a completed Frayer Model for [WORD] suitable for [GRADE] students."

Activity 2: Word Gradient (Shades of Meaning)

📋 How to Use

Create a spectrum from weak to strong:

Example - "Walk": Stroll → Walk → March → Trudge → Plod

Students place words on a continuum and discuss the nuances.

AI Prompt: "Generate a word gradient for [WORD] with 5 synonyms showing increasing intensity."

Activity 3: Semantic Mapping (Word Webs)

📋 How to Use

Start with a central word. Branch to:

  • Synonyms
  • Antonyms
  • Word family (noun, verb, adjective forms)
  • Collocations (words that go with it)
  • Examples from the text

AI Prompt: "Create a semantic map for [WORD] including synonyms, antonyms, word family, and common collocations."

Activity 4: Vocabulary in Context (Cloze)

📋 How to Use

Students fill in blanks using target words:

"The evidence seemed __________ at first, but closer inspection revealed flaws. (convincing/compelling/superficial)"

AI Prompt: "Create 5 cloze sentences using these words: [LIST]. Include a word bank and context clues."

Activity 5: Four Square Vocabulary

📋 How to Use

Students complete:

  1. Write the word
  2. Write a definition in their own words
  3. Draw a picture or symbol
  4. Write a sentence using the word

Best for: Visual learners, concrete words, interactive notebooks

The Cognate Advantage

EAL students from Romance language backgrounds (Spanish, French, Portuguese, Italian) can recognize thousands of English words through cognates. Help them activate this knowledge:

🌉 Cognate Categories

Perfect Cognates Identical spelling/meaning: animal, natural, original, secret, simple
Near Cognates Slight spelling changes: accident/accidente, education/educación, information/información
False Friends Watch out: embarazada ≠ embarrassed, actual ≠ actual, sensible ≠ sensible

🎯 Cognate-Focused Prompt

From this vocabulary list [LIST], identify cognates for Spanish speakers.

For each cognate:
- Highlight the connection
- Note any spelling pattern (-tion/-ción, -ity/-idad)
- Warn about false friends
- Provide pronunciation guide for tricky sounds

Assessment Strategies

Receptive vs. Productive Knowledge

EAL students often have receptive understanding (they recognize words when reading) before productive ability (using words in speech/writing). Assess both:

Receptive Assessment Productive Assessment
Multiple choice definitions Original sentence writing
Matching words to pictures Story using target words
Cloze with word bank Explaining word to a partner
Sorting by meaning/category Creating word associations
