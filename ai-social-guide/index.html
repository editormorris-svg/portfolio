Why This Guide Exists

I spent months researching AI tools for content creation — avatar generators, voice synthesizers, automation platforms, analytics tools. The landscape is overwhelming, and most guides are either too basic ("use ChatGPT") or too technical (deep ML papers).

This guide bridges that gap. It's organized around where you are, not what the tools do. Each section includes a concrete use scenario, recommended tools, and actionable steps. Plus, I'm documenting my own implementation so you can follow along and learn from my mistakes.

Version 1.0 covers the foundation. As I implement more tools and strategies, I'll update this page with new versions — creating a living document of the AI content creation journey.

Your First 30 Days

  1. Set up one platform (Instagram, TikTok, or YouTube Shorts)
  2. Create a simple content calendar using Notion or Google Sheets
  3. Use ChatGPT to brainstorm 20 content ideas in your niche
  4. Batch-create 5 pieces of content using Canva + CapCut
  5. Post consistently (3x per week minimum)
  6. Review analytics after 2 weeks, double down on what works

Scale-Up Strategy

  1. Audit your top-performing content — identify patterns
  2. Implement content repurposing: 1 long video → 5+ shorts/posts
  3. Set up batch recording sessions (4-6 videos in one day)
  4. Use scheduling tools to maintain presence during off-hours
  5. Build an email list (ConvertKit, Beehiiv) for direct audience connection
  6. Test one new platform per quarter based on audience research
  7. Track ROI: time spent vs. growth metrics, adjust accordingly

Building the Machine

  1. Document your brand voice, visual style, and content pillars
  2. Create a custom GPT trained on your best-performing content
  3. Build content pipelines: idea → draft → review → schedule → publish
  4. Implement AI avatar for evergreen/evergreen-adjacent content
  5. Set up Make/Zapier automations for cross-platform distribution
  6. Build analytics dashboard tracking content → business outcomes
  7. Establish SOPs and consider hiring a content manager for oversight

My Implementation: The 0604.ai Journey

"I'm building this in public. Here's my actual plan, my real constraints, and what I'm testing. Follow along, learn from my wins and failures."

I'm a 48-year-old teacher building a YouTube presence while working full-time. I don't have 40 hours a week for content. Here's how I'm using AI to punch above my weight:

🎥 Content Strategy

  • Multi-niche approach (teacher, traveler, tech, health)
  • Batch record on weekends (school PC, 7800X3D)
  • AI-assisted editing with Premiere + Descript
  • Repurpose long-form into 5+ short clips

🤖 AI Tools Testing

  • LTX Studio for AI-generated B-roll
  • ElevenLabs for voiceover variations
  • HeyGen for avatar experiments
  • MiniMax for content ideation

⚡ Automation Plans

  • Cross-platform scheduling (YouTube → Shorts → Instagram)
  • Analytics tracking in Airtable
  • Email newsletter automation
  • Community management workflows

📊 Success Metrics

  • 10K subscribers by end of 2026
  • Reduce editing time by 50% with AI
  • Maintain 2 uploads/week consistently
  • Build engaged community, not just views

Current Status (April 2026): Channel launched March 27. First two videos recorded. Building posting rhythm. Next experiment: AI avatar for "explainer" style content while keeping vlogs personal. Results will be shared in v1.1 of this guide.
